About me

I started my Ph.D. study in the Department of Electronic Engineering at Tsinghua University in 2022, advised by Associate Prof. Quanming Yao. I received my M.S. in Mathematics from Xi'an Jiaotong University and my B.S. in Mathematics from Soochow University. During my master's study, I was advised by Prof. Deyu Meng and Prof. Yao Wang. My background in mathematics and engineering informs my interest in the foundations of modern deep learning.

Research

My research studies the interplay between architecture, representation, and optimization in modern deep learning. I am especially interested in the gap between what neural networks can express in principle and how reliably they can learn, propagate information, compose capabilities, and maintain useful representations in practice. This motivation has led me from reasoning expressivity to information propagation, capability representation and composition, and training dynamics, with the goal of turning empirical phenomena into analyzable mechanisms for building more reliable, efficient, and generalizable AI systems.

Selected works are organized by the evolution of my research interests.

2022–2023

Reasoning and expressivity

Understanding Expressivity of GNN in Rule Learning

Haiquan Qiu, Yongqi Zhang, Yong Li, Quanming Yao

ICLR 2024 arXiv Code

This work asks what kinds of rules neural architectures can represent and learn. It studies the expressivity of message-passing models for rule learning and connects observed reasoning performance with analyzable structural conditions.

2023–2024

Information propagation

Graph Unitary Message Passing

Haiquan Qiu, Quanming Yao

TMLR 2026 arXiv Code

This work examines why expressivity alone is insufficient when information cannot travel effectively through a neural architecture. It studies how architectural choices shape long-range information flow and signal preservation.

2024–2025

Capability representation and composition

Superpose Task-specific Features for Model Merging

Haiquan Qiu, You Wu, Dong Li, Jianmin Guo, Quanming Yao

EMNLP 2025 (Oral) arXiv Code

This work studies model merging from a feature perspective and preserves task-specific feature directions so that a merged model can better retain multiple skills. It suggests that successful capability composition depends on keeping the underlying representations distinct and stable.

2025–2026

Training dynamics and reliability

Why Low-Precision Transformer Training Fails: An Analysis on Flash Attention

Haiquan Qiu, Quanming Yao

ICLR 2026 (Oral) arXiv Code

This work shifts from composing trained capabilities to understanding how useful representations form and fail during optimization. It explains why low-precision Transformer training can become unstable by connecting representation patterns with numerical errors in Flash Attention.
